Forest Zones

Forest zones vary by climate and biodiversity. From tropical rainforests to boreal coniferous forests, each plays a critical role in carbon absorption and biodiversity preservation.

Tropical Forest

Located near the equator with high rainfall, these forests house the richest biodiversity on Earth.

Temperate Forest

Found in regions with four seasons, temperate forests support a wide variety of plant and animal life.

Boreal Forest

Also known as Taiga, these cold-region forests are mainly composed of conifers and help regulate Earth’s climate.

Protected Species

Forests are home to thousands of species, many of which are endangered due to habitat destruction and climate change. Protecting them is our duty.

  • Jaguar - South American Rainforest
  • Red Panda - Eastern Himalayan Forests
  • Spotted Owl - North American Temperate Forest
  • Orangutan - Bornean Tropical Forest

Sustainable Practices

Eco-friendly Forestry

Community Involvement
